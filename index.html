Introduction

In the world of competitive gaming, "being carried" means letting your teammates do most of the heavy lifting while you contribute... sparingly. Whether it's due to skill gaps, bad luck, or just one of those days, being carried can happen to anyone. What matters is how you handle it—with humor, humility, and maybe a tiny bit of effort.

Here are some examples of what being carried might look like in popular games:

  • Apex Legends:
    • Your teammate racks up 12 kills and 3,000 damage while you manage a single knock and barely 200 damage.
    • They’re clutch-reviving you for the third time in one match while you’re still figuring out which gun to pick up.
  • Overwatch:
    • Your DPS teammate secures the play of the game with a perfect Dragonblade or Deadeye, while your ult is still charging because you forgot to stay in the fight.
    • The support player’s healing numbers make headlines while your tank stats look more like an apology.
  • Fortnite:
    • Your duo partner builds a fortress in 10 seconds and eliminates half the lobby, while you’re still figuring out how to rotate without taking fall damage.
    • They ping enemy locations like a pro, and you immediately give away your position by accidentally shooting the sky.

This documentation is your guide to embracing the art of being carried gracefully. From recognizing the signs to avoiding catastrophic mistakes, you’ll learn how to turn your supporting role into a comedic highlight rather than a team liability.

Recognizing When You're Being Carried

The first step in being carried gracefully is accepting the truth—you’re not the star of this show. It’s okay; even sidekicks get screen time. Here’s how to tell if you’ve been lovingly placed in the metaphorical backpack:

  • Your stats are... Inspiringly bad:
    • In Apex Legends, your teammate has a 20-bomb badge, and you’ve got a participation trophy with 87 damage.
    • In Overwatch, your kill count is so low that even the payload is more helpful than you.
    • In Fortnite, you’re the "team medic," except all you’ve done is hand over bandages while they carry the Victory Royale.
"Congratulations! You've contributed 87 damage. You're basically a warning shot."
  • You’re always the rescue mission:
    • Knocked down? No problem. Your teammate has a permanent timeshare in the Gulag or respawn beacon, just for you.
    • The number of revives you’ve received could be a mini-game stat.
"I like to think of myself as the emotional anchor. They carry me, and I weigh them down just enough for them to feel challenged."
  • The kill feed is suspiciously one-sided:
    • You’re scanning the feed like, "Wait, when did they wipe an entire squad? I was still looting the first crate."
    • The only contribution you’ve made is a really enthusiastic "Good job!" in voice chat.
"Fun fact: If your name isn’t in the kill feed, you technically can’t be blamed for anything."
  • You feel like a live tutorial:
    • Every game feels like a crash course, and your teammate is the patient instructor saying, "It’s fine, we’ve all been there," while you die to fall damage... again.
"They’re training me. Not for greatness—just for competence. Baby steps."

Recognizing that you’re being carried isn’t an insult; it’s an opportunity. You’re not dead weight—you’re comic relief, moral support, and an inspiration to keep carrying on (literally). Once you’ve acknowledged your role, you can fully embrace it with the grace and humor of a truly lovable teammate.

Clear Comms

Let’s face it, your carry isn’t expecting Shakespeare—they just need you to say something useful for once. Call out the enemy, mark some loot, or let them know when you’ve wandered off and immediately regretted it.

Keep it simple: no one wants a monologue about how you got outplayed or why “your shots definitely hit.” And please, for everyone’s sanity, don’t panic-scream into the mic—it’s a battle royale, not a horror movie.

Occasionally, toss out a “Nice shot!” or “Thanks for saving me… again.” But remember, this is a game, not a group therapy session. Stay chill, stay brief, and try not to sound like a malfunctioning GPS.

How To Not Be A Liability

Error: "Player inted by pushing a full squad solo. Result: Team wipe."

This isn’t your anime protagonist arc. Rushing 1v3 isn’t heroic—it’s selling. Stick with your squad unless you want to star in someone else’s montage.

Log Entry: "Player missed every sniper shot. Cause: Overestimated aim skill."

If your accuracy stat screams stormtrooper, switch to a weapon with spray-and-pray potential.

Warning: "Player looted too long and showed up with a P2020. Status: Unprepared."

Loot fast, fight faster. Your carry doesn’t have time for your inventory drama.

Critical Error: "Player ping-spammed, then got knocked first. Cause: Tactical irony."

Don’t yell “They’re one shot!” while being the first to drop. It’s bad optics.

In short: stay with your team, don’t oversell your skills, and stop feeding the enemy’s montage dreams.

Give Credit

"You’re the MVP for real."

"GG, you popped off!"

Simple, heartfelt, and enough to not break mental.
